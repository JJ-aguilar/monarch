In 2015 the Arlington Career Center partnered with Monarch Sister Schools Project to educate our students about the precarious status of the monarch butterfly and to implement some local solutions. Through the Sister Schools Project, classes have the opportunity to communicate using telecommunications with students who are endeavoring to plant oyamel trees in Michoacan Province, Mexico. Many of the oyamel fir trees on which monarchs overwinter have been logged over the years.

In the fall of 2015, students and staff worked together to establish a 150 sq. ft. garden, located at the corner of Highland Street and the school parking lot. Students planted the garden with milkweeds and other native nectar plants (A list of these plants can be found under the link Native Plants on this website). Students also applied for certification with Monarch Watch to designate our garden as an official “Monarch Waystation”. The first phase of the Monarch Project invited collaboration among several Arlington Career Center classes. These included:

  • Digging of garden: ACC staff and some students from the Program for Employment Preparedness (PEP) and Early Childhood Education
  • Garden Plan: HILT students from Ms. Maskelony’s geometry class
  • Garden Planting: PEP students, Arlington Community High School students, and HILT students
  • Fencing: Students in Sustainable Technologies class
  • Plant Signage: PEP students and Animal Science class

Several caterpillars also developed in cages in the ACC main office, for all students to see.

In the winter of 2016, HILT Program students assumed an important leadership role for the Project when they created a Powerpoint about the Project, which they shared via SKYPE with students in Mexico. Students took turns describing to Mexican students what we had done and showing their Power Point.

While we saw a drop in Monarch populations in the 2016-17 year, due to several storms which caused severe damage to the Biosphere, we are hopeful to find monarch eggs and larvae on our own milkweed plants in 2017. Our goal is to involve as many classes in rearing butterflies as we can.
