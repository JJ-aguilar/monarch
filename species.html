Monarchs belong to the class of animals known as insects. Monarchs belong to the Order Lepidoptera, which means "scaly wings." This order of animals incudes butterflies and moths.

Most insects eat different food as adults from the food they ate as juveniles. The reason native plants are so interinsic to the health of many pollinators is that many have evolved with specific plants, and require those plants for survival. Monarch butterflies (and may lepidoptera) feed on flowers as adults, using their long proboscises to suck nectar from the plants. Monarch caterpillars, on the other hand,are "specialists". They can only eat and develop from the leves of milkweed plants. A singnificant drop in the milkweed population/therefore, equates to a significant drop in the monarch population.

Butterfly Migration

We are working to support the butterflies as they migrate between Canada and the biosphere in central Mexico. ACC students created a garden to sustain the monarch butterflies with nectar plants and to provide milkweeds, which are the one plants the butterfly caterpillars can eat. We have a sister school in Mexico. Students there are planting oyamel trees, which are the trees the monarch overwinter on.

Our goals Are:

  • Learning about monarchs by studying them and raising them
  • Communicating with students in Mexico
  • Maintaining the garden to sustain the species
  • Educating the community to plant native plants to help the butterflies
