Classes throughout the Arlington Career Center have worked together to plant a garden that will sustain pollinators, with a focus on the monarch butterfly. The monarch butterfly makes an iconic 2,500 mile round-trip journey between Canada and Mexico every year. Due to factors like pollution, pesticides, and students have added native plants to an existing garden outside the main doors

Our goals Are:

  • Learning about monarchs by studying them and raising them
  • Communicating with students in Mexico
  • Maintaining the garden to sustain the species
  • Educating the community to plant native plants to help the butterflies
